# Background

The [HERO App (Heart Rehab Information Tool)](https://dhp.lbg.ac.at/hero-das-herz-reha-informationstool/) was originally developed as part of a PhD project to help patients after a heart event, like a heart attack or surgery, navigate their way through cardiac rehabilitation. It provides easy-to-understand digital information about the next steps in treatment and helps fill information gaps after hospital care.

Today, the app is being further developed for additional healthcare use cases under the name HERO WAYS as part of follow-up projects such as [Prehab2Rehab](https://prehab2rehab.at/). What started as a cardiology-focused tool is evolving into a modular, flexibly adaptable platform. The aim is to extend the application to other medical contexts, for example visceral surgical procedures, thereby creating a sustainable and transferable solution for a wide range of healthcare areas.

The development is led by the [Ludwig Boltzmann Institute for Digital Health and Prevention](https://dhp.lbg.ac.at/) and has been supported by the [Paris Lodron University of Salzburg](https://www.plus.ac.at/?lang=en). The project was funded by the [Open Innovation in Science Center of the Ludwig Boltzmann Gesellschaft](https://lbg.ac.at/forschung/open-innovation-in-science/). Technical implementation is carried out in collaboration with the industry partner [Redox Interactive](https://redox-interactive.com/).
